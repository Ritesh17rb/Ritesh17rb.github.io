![A quote from Vinland Saga over a still frame of Thors standing in a field: "You have no enemies. No one has any enemies." — Thors](/blog/assets/vinland-saga-thors-quote.webp)

I was watching Vinland Saga, half paying attention, phone in one hand, when Thors said this line and I had to pause the episode.

![Screenshot of Vinland Saga paused on Netflix, S2:E20 "Pain", taken from a phone camera showing the laptop screen and keyboard](/blog/assets/watching-vinland-saga.webp)

*You have no enemies. No one has any enemies. There is no one who it's okay to hurt.*

I sat with that for a long time. Longer than I've sat with most things this year. Because it flipped a question I'd been asking myself for years — "why do people treat me like this, what's wrong with me" — into a completely different one. Maybe it was never about me at all.

## The chasing

For a long time, I did this thing where I'd chase people. Not romantically — just, people in general. Friends, groups, anyone whose approval felt like it would finally make me feel like I belonged somewhere.

It works like a butterfly effect. You try a little harder to fit into one group, it doesn't land, so you try harder with the next one. That one wobbles too, so you chase a friendship to make up for it. Somewhere in that chain reaction, you stop noticing that you've rewritten yourself five times over trying to be liked by people who were never going to like you no matter what shape you took.

And I lost myself doing it. Genuinely. There were versions of me that existed only to be palatable to a specific friend group, and none of those versions were actually me.

## The nights I don't talk about much

I used to cry — full nights, not exaggerating — over friends who clearly didn't value me. I'd replay conversations, look for the moment I said the wrong thing, the thing that made them pull away. I tried *everything* to fit in. Changed how I spoke, what I laughed at, how much space I took up in a room. None of it was worth what it cost.

I've lost a lot of friends over the years. I'm not going to pretend that doesn't hurt, because it does, even now, even knowing everything I know now. But somewhere in the losing, I started noticing a pattern: the people who dropped me, or made me feel small, or went cold for no reason I could ever pin down — it was rarely ever actually about me.

## It was never about me

This is the part that took the longest to actually believe, not just say out loud.

How people treat you has almost everything to do with *them* — their insecurity, their own unresolved stuff, whatever they're carrying that has nothing to do with you — and almost nothing to do with you. You can drive yourself into the ground trying to fix something on your end that was never broken. The bug was never in your code.

Thors' line is the same idea from a different angle. If no one is truly an enemy, if no one is ever really "okay to hurt" — then the flip side is also true: you were never actually the problem to be solved. People carrying their own war inside them will hurt whoever's closest, and closest just happened to be you. It's not a verdict on your worth. It's just where they were standing when it spilled over.

## What I do now instead

You cannot change what people think of you. You cannot change *them*. And here's the part I had to really accept — you don't have to. Not your job, not your job, not your job.

So now I just try to live myself, fully, and let people think whatever they want to think. If they like it, good. If they don't, also good, honestly — at least it's the real thing they're reacting to and not some edited version of me built to be agreeable.

![Selfie, casual, no filter, just existing](/blog/assets/ritesh-selfie.webp)

Live like everyone already loves you. Not in a delusional way — in the way where you stop auditioning for it. Walk into the room like you belong there, because you do, because everyone does, because apparently even Thors thinks so.

I still have days where an old friendship crosses my mind and stings a little. That's fine. It's allowed to still hurt sometimes. But I'm not chasing anymore. I'm just here, being whoever I actually am, and that's finally enough.
